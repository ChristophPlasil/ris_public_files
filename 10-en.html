Dedicated to River information Service experts, this website facilitates access to a knowledge base for inland navigation information technologies.

Since 2009, RIS experts have been involved in the European harmonisation of information technologies since modern traffic management systems allow real-time data exchange between vessels and shore.

In the context, the enlargement of CESNI’s activities in the field of information technologies in 2019, the CESNI Committee established, by its resolution 2018-II-16, a permanent working group on information technologies (CESNI/TI). The mission of CESNI/TI is specified in the annex to the resolution and the new working group is at the core of CESNI’s activities in the field of information technologies. Accordingly, the CESNI Committee has tasked its Working group CESNI/TI to develop proposals by autumn 2019 for the gradual integration of the existing RIS Expert Groups in CESNI’s structure and procedures by 1 January 2020.

CESNI members see major benefits from progressive integrating the RIS expert groups into CESNI’s structure and processes. During 2019, CESNI/TI working Group, in close collaboration with these expert groups, has therefore initially concentrated on making this integration a success.

The 15th of October 2019, the CESNI Committee resolved to integrate the existing RIS expert groups into the structure and procedures of CESNI in the form of four temporary working groups each dedicated to the RIS technologies.

The technical work towards standardization of four RIS key-technologies is performed by experts organised in four temporary working Groups.

The temporary working Groups produce the standards and updates, but also technical clarification documents, guidelines and other relevant documents on RIS key-technologies.

The temporary working Groups are : composed with experts with a high level of expertise related to RIS and especially in the field of each of the key technologies, designated by the Members of CESNI referred to in Article 2 paragraphs, 1, 2 and 3 letter a, b, and c of the Rules of Procedure of the CESNI.

The work of the temporary working Groups is based on their mandates, specific for each temporary working Group.

There are four temporary working Groups for each of the RIS key-technologies:

  • Inland Electronic Chart Display and Information Systems (Inland ECDIS)

  • Electronic Reporting International (ERI)

  • Notices to Skippers (NtS)

  • Vessel Tracking and Tracing (VTT)
